in the ganges there is an island of very considerable size, inhabited by a single nation; it is called modogalinga. beyond the ganges are situate the modubae, the molindae, the uberae, with a magnificent city of the same name, the modresi, the preti, the caloae, the sasuri, the passalae, the colobae, the orumcolae, the abali, and the thalutae. the king of the last-named people has fifty thousand foot-soldiers, four thousand horse, and four hundred armed elephants. we next come to a still more powerful nation, the andarae, who dwell in numerous villages, as well as thirty cities fortified with walls and towers. they furnish for their king one hundred thousand foot, two thousand horse, and a thousand elephants. the country of the dardae is the most productive of gold, that of the setae of silver.

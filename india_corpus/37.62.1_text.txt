the 'lepidotis,' or 'scaly stone,' mimics fish scales in various colours, while the 'lesbias,' or 'stone of lesbos,' resembles a clod of earth. it takes its name from its country of origin, but is found also in india. the 'leucophthalmos,' or 'white eye,' which is otherwise reddish, includes an eye-shaped layer which is white and black. the 'lencopoecilos,' or 'variegated white stone,' has a white ground marked with drops of vermilion mixed with gold. the 'libanochrus,' or 'colour-of-incense,' shows a resemblance to frankincense and gives off a honey-coloured streak. the 'limoniatis,' or 'meadow stone,' seems to be identical with the 'smaragdus.'
